AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine

All COVID-19 vaccines authorized in Canada are proven safe, effective and of high quality.

Name: AstraZeneca COVID-19 Vaccine

Manufacturer: AstraZeneca Canada Inc and Verity/SII (COVISHIELD)

Type: Viral vector-based

Status: Approved by Health Canada

Approved for: Age 18 and older

How it's given: Injection in the muscle of the upper arm

Number of doses: 2 doses

Who should get vaccinated

The vaccine is approved for people who are 18 years of age and older. Its safety and effectiveness in people younger than 18 years of age have not yet been established.

How to get vaccinated near you

When to consult with your doctor

Consult with your doctor or health care provider if you:

  • have questions or concerns about vaccination
  • are unsure if you should receive the COVID-19 vaccine
  • are immunocompromised due to a disease or treatment, such as chemotherapy
  • have an autoimmune condition
  • are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • have a severe allergy to an ingredient in the COVID-19 vaccine
  • have experienced mild to moderate immediate allergic reactions after a previous dose of an authorized COVID-19 vaccine
  • are experiencing any symptoms of COVID-19
  • have a bleeding disorder

Effectiveness and dosage

Vaccines protect you by:

  • reducing the community transmission of the virus
  • reducing your risk of contracting COVID-19
  • reducing your risk of serious illness from COVID-19

Effectiveness in clinical trials

Clinical trials showed that, beginning 2 weeks after the second dose, the AstraZeneca COVID-19 was about 62% effective against COVID-19.

Vaccine dosage

This vaccine requires 2 doses for maximum protection.

The vaccination schedule approved by Health Canada is to give the two doses 4 to 12 weeks apart, based on evidence from clinical trials.

Your province or territory decides when people receive their first and second doses of the vaccine. Your second dose may be delayed to allow more people to get their first dose.

These decisions are based on public health recommendations and the latest evidence.

Possible side effects

After getting vaccinated, it's common and normal to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few days after vaccination.

This is the body's natural response, as it's working hard to build protection against the disease.

Common vaccine side effects may include:

Symptoms at the injection site, such as: Flu-like symptoms, such as:
  • redness
  • soreness
  • swelling
  • chills
  • fatigue
  • joint pain
  • headache
  • mild fever
  • muscle aches

Allergic reactions

This vaccine doesn't contain common food allergens, such as eggs, shellfish, gluten or nuts.

If you know you have allergies to any of the ingredients in the AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine, you should not receive it:

  • Ingredient list

If you have experienced a serious allergic reaction to another vaccine, drug or food, you should talk to your health professional before you receive the vaccine.

Reports of extremely rare blood clots

Very rare cases of blood clots with low platelets have been reported following vaccination with this vaccine. For most people, the risks associated with getting COVID-19 far outweigh the risks of a blood clot.

Blood clots are treatable. Early diagnosis can reduce complications. Seek medical attention if you experience symptoms of blood clots, like shortness of breath, chest pain, leg swelling, or neurological symptoms like persistent headaches.

Until we better understand the link between these rare blood clots events and the vaccine, your province or territory may decide to not give this vaccine, or to restrict the age groups that can receive it.

These decisions are based on public health recommendations and the latest evidence.

Vaccine review and approval

Health Canada's independent drug review process is recognized around the world for its high standards and rigor. Our decisions are based only on scientific and medical evidence showing that vaccines are safe and effective. The benefits must also outweigh any risks.
