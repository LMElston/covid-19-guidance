How COVID-19 vaccines work

There are 2 main types of vaccines: viral vector-based and mRNA vaccines.

  • Traditional viral vector-based vaccines

    These types of vaccines use a harmless virus (in this case, the adenovirus) as a delivery system. Just like with a natural infection, when the immune cells in the body are exposed to parts of the virus in a vaccine, antibodies are developed and immune cells are primed to respond to prevent infection.

    • viral vector-based vaccines
  • New mRNA vaccines

    They are a new type of vaccine that don’t contain viruses or bacteria.

    RNA (ribonucleic accid) vaccines contain the instructions for making the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein. This protein is found on the surface of the virus that causes COVID-19.

    Our immune system recognizes that the protein doesn't belong there and begins building an immune response and making antibodies.

    mRNA vaccines don’t modify people’s DNA.

    • mRNA vaccines
