Vaccines for COVID-19: What to expect when you get vaccinated

Like any medication or supplement (including vitamins), vaccines can cause side effects and reactions.

Safety of authorized COVID-19 vaccine

There continues to be no vaccine safety issues identified or any unusual characteristics of adverse events following administration of COVID-19 vaccines.

Each vaccine developed for COVID-19 may have different ingredients.

Go to the authorized vaccine page for more information on ingredients, allergies, side effects, how they work and how they’re administered:

Common vaccine side effects and reactions

After being vaccinated, it's common to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few weeks after vaccination.

Common vaccine side effects may include:

  • mild fever
  • flu-like symptoms, such as:
    • chills
    • fatigue
    • joint pain
    • headache
    • muscle aches
  • redness, soreness or swelling in the arm or leg where the vaccine was given

As with all vaccines, there’s a chance that there will be a serious side effect, but these are rare. Serious side effects could be something like an allergic reaction, a fever and chills.

Vaccine safety, concerns, and possible side effects

Reporting safety concerns for vaccines

Anyone who witnesses or experiences a possible reaction to a vaccine is encouraged to report it to their health care provider.

COVID-19 vaccine safety: Weekly report on possible reactions following vaccination

If a safety issue is found to be related to immunization, Health Canada will take appropriate action. This could include updating the product information, communicating new risks to Canadians and healthcare providers or changing the recommended use of the product.

After getting vaccinated

Until a large number of people across Canada are vaccinated, public health measures will continue to be essential to protect everyone and minimize the spread of COVID-19.

At the moment, getting vaccinated doesn’t affect travel restrictions and quarantine requirements.
