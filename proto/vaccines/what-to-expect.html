Authorized vaccines

You will be given one of the vaccines authorized for use in Canada.

Each vaccine developed for COVID-19 may have different ingredients.

Go the vaccine page for information on ingredients, allergies, side effects, how they work and how they’re administered:

  • AstraZeneca / COVISHIELD
  • Janssen (Johnson & Johnson)
  • Moderna
  • Pfizer-BioNTech

Side effects and reactions

After getting vaccinated, it's common to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few weeks after vaccination.

Common side effects

Common side effects
In the arm where you got the shot Throughout the rest of your body
  • redness
  • soreness
  • swelling
  • chills
  • fatigue
  • joint pain
  • headache
  • muscle aches

Tips to reduce discomfort

You can take medicine after your vaccination to help with any pain or to lower a fever. Ask your health care provider what they recommend.

You can also:

  • apply a clean, cool, wet washcloth over the area you received the shot
  • use or exercise your arm
  • drink plenty of fluids

Rare severe reactions

Other rare reactions that could occur after a vaccine include:

  • a fever above 40°C (104°F)
  • swelling and an itchy rash where the vaccine was given
  • an allergic (anaphylactic) reaction

Reporting an adverse reaction

If you witness or experience a reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine, report it to your health care provider.

After getting vaccinated

Until a large number of people across Canada are vaccinated, public health measures will continue to be essential to protect everyone and minimize the spread of COVID-19.

At the moment, getting vaccinated doesn’t affect travel restrictions and quarantine requirements.
