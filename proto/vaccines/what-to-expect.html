Authorized vaccines

Health Canada has authorized four vaccines for use in Canada. Not all of the authorized vaccines are available to all areas of the country.

All approved vaccines are proven safe and effective.

Tips for a comfortable vaccination experience

There are a number of techniques you can use to make the vaccination more comfortable and limit pain or discomfort from being vaccinated.

Some helpful strategies include:

  • sit upright during vaccination
  • wear a short-sleeved or loose-fitting top
  • relax your arm – let it feel loose and jiggly like cooked spaghetti
  • use deep breathing to help you relax and feel calm
  • if you feel dizzy or faint let your healthcare provider know
  • distract yourself by reading or listening to music, or have a conversation
  • talk to your health care provider ahead of time about pain relief that you could take

Side effects and reactions

After getting vaccinated, it's common and normal to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few days after vaccination.

This is the body's natural response, as it's working hard to build immunity against the disease. This is called an inflammatory response or reaction.

Common side effects

Common side effects
In the arm where you got the shot Throughout the rest of your body
  • redness
  • soreness
  • swelling
  • chills
  • fatigue
  • joint pain
  • headache
  • mild fever
  • muscle aches

Tips to reduce discomfort

You can take medicine after your vaccination to help with any pain or to lower a fever. Ask your health care provider what they recommend.

You can also:

  • apply a clean, cool, wet washcloth over the area you received the shot
  • use or exercise your arm
  • drink plenty of fluids

Rare serious reactions

Other rare reactions that could occur after a vaccine include:

  • a fever above 40°C (104°F)
  • swelling and an itchy rash where the vaccine was given
  • an allergic (anaphylactic) reaction

Reporting a possible serious reaction

If you witness or experience a possible serious reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine, report it to your health care provider.

After getting vaccinated

After vaccination, ask for a record of any vaccines you receive.

Currently, it isn’t yet known whether the spread of COVID-19 can be stopped by vaccination alone. Because of this, it is essential that everyone continues to follow public health measures to help stop the spread of COVID-19 in our communities.

At the moment, getting vaccinated doesn’t affect travel restrictions and quarantine requirements.
