Before getting your vaccine

When to consult with your doctor

For some people, the decision to get vaccinated will require consideration of risks versus benefits.

You should consult your doctor or a health professional if you:

  • are immunocompromised due to a disease or treatment, such as chemotherapy
  • have an autoimmune condition
  • are pregnant or breastfeeding

What vaccine will you get

There are currently no vaccine safety issues identified or any unusual characteristics of adverse events related to COVID-19 vaccines.

Each vaccine developed for COVID-19 may have different ingredients.

Go to the authorized vaccine page for more information on ingredients, allergies, side effects, how they work and how they’re administered:

Common side effects and reactions to all vaccines

After getting vaccinated, it's common to have temporary side effects. These can last a few hours to a few weeks after vaccination.

Common vaccine side effects may include:

  • mild fever
  • flu-like symptoms, such as:
    • chills
    • fatigue
    • joint pain
    • headache
    • muscle aches
  • redness, soreness or swelling in the arm or leg where the vaccine was given

As with all vaccines, there’s also a chance that there will be a serious side effect, but these are rare. Serious side effects could be something like an allergic reaction, a fever and chills.

If you have an adverse reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine

If you witness or experience a reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine, report it to your health care provider. They are required to report it to their local public health authority. If there is a safety issue with COVID-19 vaccines, Health Canada will take appropriate action.

You can take medicine to help with any pain or to lower a fever. Ask your health care provider what they recommend.

After getting vaccinated

Until a large number of people across Canada are vaccinated, public health measures will continue to be essential to protect everyone and minimize the spread of COVID-19.

At the moment, getting vaccinated doesn’t affect travel restrictions and quarantine requirements.
