Vaccination plans in your province or territory

Provinces and territories are responsible for the vaccination rollout plans for their residents, including:

  • which groups are getting vaccinated, in what order
  • when vaccinations will be available
  • where vaccinations will be administered

Who is eligible for the vaccine

Vaccines will be available to everyone in Canada including those who aren't citizens. This applies to:

  • people over the age of:
    • 16 for the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine
    • 18 for the Moderna vaccine
  • Canadian Armed Forces personnel that are on active duty abroad
  • diplomatic staff and their dependants

When to consult with your doctor

For some people, the decision to get vaccinated will require consideration of risks versus benefits.

You should consult your doctor or a health professional if you:

  • have suppressed immunity due to a disease or treatment, such as chemotherapy
  • have an autoimmune condition
  • are pregnant or breastfeeding

Recommended priority groups

The National Advisory Committee on Immunization (NACI) has provided guidelines on who should get vaccinated first.

Your province or territory has the authority to decide who will be prioritized.

At-risk groups identified by NACI
  • residents and staff of shared living settings that provide care for seniors
  • adults 70 years of age and older, beginning with adults 80 years of age and older, and then decreasing the age limit by 5-year increments to age 70 years as supply becomes available
  • health care workers who have direct contact with patients, including those who work in health care settings and personal support workers
  • adults in Indigenous communities

As additional COVID-19 vaccines and supplies become available, the following populations should be offered vaccinations:

  • health care workers not included in the initial rollout
  • residents and staff of all other shared living settings, such as homeless shelters, correctional facilities, and housing for migrant workers
  • essential workers who face additional risks to maintain services for the functioning of society
