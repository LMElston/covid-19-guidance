Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to receive the CRHP, you must meet both of the following criteria:

  1. You meet all the eligibility requirements of the CEWS

    Employers who are not eligible for the CEWS are not eligible for the CRHP.

    Who can apply for the CEWS
    • You had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020
    • You are an eligible employer
    • You experienced the required revenue drop to be eligible for the CEWS

    The amount your revenue has dropped is determined by comparing your eligible revenue during the crisis with your eligible revenue from a specific time before March 15, 2020.

    Revenue drop required to qualify for each CEWS claim period
    Claim period Revenue drop required
    17 (June 6 to July 3, 2021) More than 0%
    18 (July 4 to July 31, 2021) More than 10%
    19 (August 1 to August 28, 2021) More than 10%
    20 (August 29 to September 25, 2021) More than 10%
    21 (September 26 to October 23, 2021) More than 10%
    22 (October 23 to November 20, 2021) More than 10%

    The revenue drop is only calculated once each claim period as part of your CEWS calculation, but CRHP requires the same eligible drop in revenue.

    Use the online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet to find your revenue drop while calculating how much CRHP or CEWS you may receive.

    For more detail on eligible employers and eligibility criteria, see Who can apply for the CEWS

  2. You are not a for-profit corporation except the following

    Most types of employers who are eligible for CEWS will be eligible for CRHP, except for some corporations and partnerships:

    Type of employer Eligible for
    For-profit corporations other than:
    • Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs)
    • cooperative corporations that are eligible for the small business deduction
    		 Not eligible for CRHP. Only CEWS.
    Partnerships where at least 50% of the partnership’s interests are held (directly or indirectly) by employers eligible for CEWS but not CRHP Not eligible for CRHP. Only CEWS.

Confirming your eligibility

If we notice a mistake in your application, we will work with you to correct it. If you realize you made a mistake in your application, you can request a change to your previous claim.

We will review your claim and confirm the information you submit.
