Number of active eligible employees Get help for:Number of active employees

Number of active employees

On Line 400, enter the number of active employees you had on your payroll during the period covered by this claim.

On Line 410, enter the number of active employees you had on your payroll during the base period (March 14 to April 10, 2021).

Do not include employees on leave with pay. Only enter the number of Opens in a new window.employees that meet the eligibility criteria.

If in a claim period an employee was active for at least one week and also on leave with pay for at least one week, count each week they were active as 0.25 of an employee. For example: four employees were “active” for week 1, and were “on leave with pay” for weeks 2, 3, and 4. Each employee would be counted as 0.25 active employees (0.25 x 1-week). Adding the four employees together, you would enter “1” (0.25 + 0.25 + 0.25 + 0.25) in the box for Line 400 (or Line 410, as applicable).

Total eligible remuneration paid to active eligible employees Get help for:Total eligible remuneration paid to active employees

Total eligible remuneration paid to active eligible employees

To calculate Line 420 and Line 430, use the spreadsheet or online calculator found on the Opens in a new window.Calculate your CEWS or CRHP amount page.

On Line 420, enter the total eligible remuneration paid to active employees for the claim period, up to a maximum of $1,129 per week, per employee, and subject to the baseline remuneration for non-arm’s length employees. Do not include remuneration paid to employees on leave with pay.

On Line 430, enter the total eligible remuneration paid to active employees for the base period, up to a maximum of $1,129 per week, per employee, and subject to the baseline remuneration for non-arm’s length employees. Do not include remuneration paid to employees on leave with pay.

Eligible remuneration includes amounts you paid an employee as salary, wages and other taxable benefits, fees, and commissions. These are amounts employers would be required to make payroll deductions on to be remitted to the CRA.

Severance pay and items such as stock option benefits or the personal use of a corporate vehicle are not part of eligible remuneration.
