Why there are two subsidies

The Canada Recovery Hiring Program (CRHP) and Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) both support wages you pay to staff as you continue to be affected by the COVID-19 pandemic.

While the CEWS amount generally increases the more your revenue has dropped, CRHP supports you as your wage-related expenses grow, whether it’s due to hiring staff, increasing shifts, or increasing overall pay:

  • CEWS
  • CRHP

CEWSOption 1 of 2

Based on how much your revenue has dropped × eligible employee pay

CRHPOption 2 of 2

Based on a flat rate × how much your overall pay to eligible employees is increasing

Which subsidy to apply for

The CRHP and CEWS are both available for claim periods 17 to 20. If you are eligible, apply for whichever subsidy gives you the higher amount:

  • you can apply for a different subsidy each claim period

  • you cannot:
    • receive both subsidies in one claim period
    • split your claim between subsidies

The online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet calculates both subsidies for claim periods 17 to 20. It will show you both results, so you can see the higher amount for your situation.

If you apply for the CRHP, you will be asked to enter the amount of CEWS you would be eligible for at line 300 in the application form.

We use this line in the application to:

  • confirm your eligibility for the CRHP
  • confirm that you are applying for the subsidy that gives you the higher amount

What time periods the subsidies cover

CRHP and CEWS are both based on four-week claim periods. The two subsidies overlap for four of these claim periods, from June 6 to September 25, 2021 (periods 17 to 20). For these claim periods you can apply for either CRHP or CEWS, whichever you are eligible for and gives you the higher amount.

Claim period Apply for
1 to 16 (October 25, 2020 to June 5, 2021) Only CEWS
17 to 20 (June 6 to September 25, 2021) Either CEWS or CRHP
21 and 22 (September 26 to November 20, 2021) Only CRHP

Who is eligible to apply

Most of the eligibility criteria for CRHP and CEWS are the same, including payroll account requirements and required revenue drops.

Most types of employers who are eligible for CEWS will be eligible for CRHP, except for some corporations and partnerships:

Type of employer Apply for
For-profit corporations other than:
  • Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs)
  • cooperative corporations that are eligible for the small business deduction
 Only CEWS
Partnerships where at least 50% of the partnership’s interests are held (directly or indirectly) by employers eligible for CEWS but not CRHP Only CEWS

Calculation similarities and differences

Revenue drop

Your revenue drop only needs to be calculated once each claim period, whether you claim CEWS or CRHP.

CEWS uses your revenue drop percentage to calculate your subsidy and uses it to determine if you qualify for the subsidy for some periods.

CRHP only uses your revenue drop percentage to determine if you qualify for the subsidy.

The online calculator or spreadsheet will ask for the information necessary to calculate your claim, and will determine which subsidy will give you the higher amount.
