    How are payments calculated

    Every month of the benefit year (July to June of the following year), Canada child benefit payments are adjusted based on:

    • the number of children in your care
    • the age of your children
    • your marital status
    • your adjusted family net income (AFNI), as reported in last year's tax return

Math behind the calculation

For each claim (qualifying) period, the amount of CRHP equals:

your incremental remuneration

multiplied by

the CRHP subsidy rate for the period

There is no overall limit on the total subsidy amount that an eligible employer may claim.

You can use the online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet to find the amount of CRHP or CEWS you may claim.

Incremental remuneration calculation

Your incremental remuneration for a claim period is how much your overall pay (eligible remuneration) for all of your actively working eligible employees has increased since the period of March 14 to April 10, 2021, (CEWS claim period 14).

Find this amount for each claim period by calculating A − B, where:

A
is the total of the following amounts—for each eligible employee, for each week in the claim period, the least of the following:
  • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • $1129, or
  • if the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee, the pre-crisis remuneration of the employee determined for that week
B
is the total of the following amounts—for each eligible employee, for each week from March 14 to April 10, 2021, the least of the following:
  • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • $1129, or
  • if the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee, the pre-crisis remuneration of the employee determined for that week

Subsidy rates for each claim period

Each claim period uses a flat rate to calculate the subsidy amount:

Claim period Subsidy rate
17 (June 6 to July 3, 2021) 50%
18 (July 4 to July 31, 2021) 50%
19 (August 1 to August 28, 2021) 50%
20 (August 29 to September 25, 2021) 40%
21 (September 26 to October 23, 2021) 30%
22 (October 23 to November 20, 2021) 20%
