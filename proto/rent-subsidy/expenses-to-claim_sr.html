Expenses you can claim

On this page

  • Qualifying property
  • Eligible expenses

The CERS covers a portion of eligible expenses in respect of a claim period for each qualifying property, subject to certain maximums. The CERS is calculated on a property by property basis.

Qualifying property

Properties (business locations) that do qualify, include any "real or immovable property" (buildings or land) in Canada that your business or organization:

  • owns or rents, and
  • uses in the course of your ordinary business activities

Properties that do not qualify, include those that are:

  • owned by you and primarily used to earn rental income from arm’s length parties, or
  • used as a residence by the business owner, family members, or other non-arm’s-length persons (for example, their home or cottage)

Eligible expenses

There are a few criteria expenses need to meet, in order to be eligible to be included in your claim for a particular claim period.

  • Only amounts paid to an arm’s length party can be included
  • The expense must be in respect of the claim period
  • The expense must be paid under a written agreement in place before October 9, 2020 (or a renewal on similar terms or assignment of such an agreement)
  • You must have already paid the eligible expenses before you submit a claim for a period
  • You cannot claim expenses that:
    • have not yet been paid
    • were paid for a timeframe that falls outside of the claim period you are applying for

For each claim period, you can claim eligible expenses up to a maximum of:

  • $75,000 per business location (base and top-up)
  • $300,000 in total for all locations (including any amounts claimed by affiliated businesses)
    • applies to the base subsidy only
    • there is no maximum for the top-up subsidy

Eligible expenses if you rent the qualifying property

Learn more about your eligible expenses if you rent the qualifying property

If you rent the qualifying property, your eligible expenses are:

  • Rent (including rent based on a percentage of sales, profit or similar criteria)
  • Amounts required to be paid by you under a net lease (either to the lessor or a third party). Includes:
    • Base rent
    • Regular payments for customary operating expenses
    • Property and similar taxes
    • Regular payments to the lessor for customary ancillary services

Any amount included in your claim in respect of the above expenses must exclude:

  • Sales taxes (such as GST/HST and provincial sales taxes)
  • Amounts in respect of damages
  • Interest or penalties on unpaid amounts
  • Other special amounts

Note that if your lessor (landlord) received an amount under the Canada Emergency Commercial Rent Assistance (CECRA) program in relation to a prior period, and instead of reimbursing you directly, applied the amount to future rent payments, you can still claim the full rent amount in the current period even though part of the rent amount is offset by such a reimbursement.

Eligible expenses if you own the qualifying property

Learn more about your eligible expenses if you own the qualifying property

If you own the qualifying property, your eligible expenses are:

  • Property and similar taxes
    • Includes school taxes and municipal taxes, if these are part of your property tax assessment
  • Property insurance
  • Interest on commercial mortgages for the purpose of purchasing real property

If you earned any revenue from sub-leasing space on the property from arm’s length parties, you must subtract that revenue from your eligible expenses.

Expenses that are not eligible include:

  • payments between non-arm’s length entities
  • amounts that have not been paid yet
  • amounts that were paid for a time that fall outside of the claim period
