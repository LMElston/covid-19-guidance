Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP)

As a Canadian employer in the tourism or hospitality industry who has been affected by COVID-19, you may be eligible for wage and rent subsidies through the Tourism and Hospitality.

Who can apply for the TRHP

There are two ways eligible organizations can qualify for the TRHP:

Tourism and hospitality sector: Option 1 of 2

Your organization:

  • is part of the tourism and hospitality industry
  • has an average monthly revenue reduction from March 2020 to Feruary 2021 of at least 40%
  • has a current-month revenue drop of at least 40%

Public health lockdown during the claim period types: Option 2 of 2

You were affected by a public health lockdown that:

  • affected at least one location for 7 days or more within the claim period
  • prevented activities that account for at least 25% of your business's revenues

Your organization:

  • has a current-month revenue drop of at least 40%

Subsidies you may qualify for under the THRP

Organizations that meet the qualifications for the THRP program will qualify for preferential rates for the wage and rent subsidies.

Wage and payroll support

The wage subsidy will enable you to re-hire workers previously laid off as a result of COVID-19, help prevent further job losses, and better position you to resume normal operations following the crisis.

Rent and mortgage support

The rent subsidy will cover part of their commercial rent or property expenses and will be provided directly to the qualifying renter or property owner, not the landlord.

Average 12 month revenue decline

Eligible organizations would also be required to demonstrate they were deeply affected by the pandemic by meeting the following two conditions to qualify for this program:

  1. An average monthly revenue reduction of at least 40 percent over the first 13 qualifying periods for the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (12-month revenue decline); and
  2. A current-month revenue loss of at least 40 percent.

The 12-month revenue decline would be calculated as the average of all revenue decline percentages for eligible organizations from March 2020 to February 2021 (claim periods 1-13, excluding claim period 10 or 11). Any periods in which an entity was not carrying on its ordinary operations for reasons other than a public health restriction (for example, because it is a seasonal business) would be excluded from this calculation. The existing rules would continue to apply for the purposes of calculating the current-month revenue decline.
