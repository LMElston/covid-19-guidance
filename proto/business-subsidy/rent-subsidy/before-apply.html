Gather the information you’ll need to calculate your amounts and apply for the subsidy

For each qualifying property, you will need the:

  • amounts you have paid for all eligible expenses
  • property address
  • name and contact information of your
    • mortgage holder if you have a mortgage on the property
    • landlord if you rent the property

The application form will accept information for up to 3 properties.

If you have more than 3, enter information for the 3 properties with the highest eligible expenses.

Keep the information for all properties in your records in case we ask for it at a later date.

For affiliated entities that have claimed, or will be claiming the CERS for the same claim period, you will need the:

  • number of affiliated entities in the agreement
  • percentage assigned to you under the agreement with your affiliated entities
  • business number of each affiliated entity
  • percentage assigned to each affiliated entity under the agreement

    • The application form will accept information for up to 10 affiliated entities.

    If you have more than 10, enter information for the 10 entities with the highest assigned percentages.

    Keep the complete list in your records in case we as for it at a later date.

    Learn more about affiliated entities

    If you are affiliated with other businesses or organizations who are also applying for CERS, you must agree how you will split the rent subsidy.

    This agreement must be in writing, and you must keep it with your records.

    If the combined eligible expenses of your affiliated entities is $300,000 or more, the subsidy will be split according to the agreement. If the group’s eligible expenses do not reach the maximum, each organization will receive the full amount they are entitled to.

    An affiliated entity is an organization that:

    • controls another entity (directly or indirectly)
    • is controlled by another entity (directly or indirectly)
    • is under common control alongside another entity

    Although the rules can be complicated, some examples include:

    • you run your business activities through more than one entity (corporations, trusts or partnerships)
    • you and your spouse each own or operate a business, directly or through a corporation or other entity
    • the entity through which your business or activities are conducted is owned or controlled by people who own or control other entities
