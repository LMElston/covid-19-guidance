Why there are two subsidies

The Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS) and Canada Recovery Hiring Program (CRHP) both support the wages you pay to staff as you continue to be affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. These subsidies are designed to cover you through different phases of your economic recovery:

  • CEWS
  • CRHP

CEWSOption 1 of 2

Mainly based on how much your revenue has dropped

CRHPOption 2 of 2

Mainly based on how much your pay to employees is increasing

Which subsidy to apply for

You may only apply for either the CEWS or CRHP each claim period from 17 to 20, whichever gives you the higher amount:

  • you can apply for a different subsidy each claim period

  • you cannot apply for both in one claim period, or split your claim between subsidies

The online calculator or downloadable spreadsheet will calculate both subsidies for claim periods 17 to 20. It will show you both results, so you can see the higher amount for your situation.

What time periods the subsidies cover

CEWS and CRHP are both based on four-week claim periods. The two subsidies overlap for four of these claim periods, from June 6 to September 25, 2021 (periods 17 to 20). For these claim periods you can apply to either CRHP or CEWS, whichever you are eligible for and gives you the higher amount.

Claim period Apply for
9 to 16 (October 25, 2020 to June 5, 2021) Only CEWS
17 to 20 (June 6 to September 25, 2021) Either CEWS or CRHP
21 and 22 (September 26 to November 20, 2021) Only CRHP

Claim periods 1 to 8 are now closed for application.

Who is eligible to apply

Most of the eligibility criteria for CEWS and CRHP are the same, including payroll account requirements and eligible revenue drops.

The same types of employers can apply for both CEWS and CRHP, except:

Type of employer Apply for
Publicly-listed eligible corporations Only CEWS
Eligible corporations that are not Canadian-controlled Only CEWS
Eligible Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs) or cooperatives that are eligible for the small business deduction Either CEWS or CRHP

What types of employees can be included in your claim

Eligible employees who actively worked throughout the claim period can be included in your CEWS or CRHP claim. Employees who were on leave with pay (furloughed) cannot be included in your CRHP claim, and can only be included in CEWS claims for certain claim periods.

Claim period Active eligible employees Eligible employees on leave with pay
17 (June 6 to July 3, 2021) CEWS or CRHP CEWS only
18 (July 4 to July 31, 2021) CEWS or CRHP CEWS only
19 (August 1 to August 28, 2021) CEWS or CRHP CEWS only
20 (August 29 to September 25, 2021) CEWS or CRHP Neither CEWS nor CRHP

Active eligible employees include both arm’s-length and non-arm’s-length eligible employees, although the amount you get for those employees may be different.

Summary of calculation similarities and differences

Revenue drop

Both subsidies calculate your revenue drop the same way. They use the same:

  • comparison months for each claim period
  • types of eligible revenue
  • deeming rule
  • revenue-related elections

CEWS uses your revenue drop percentage to calculate your subsidy rate, as well as using it to determine if you qualify for the subsidy for some periods.

CRHP only uses your revenue drop percentage to determine if you qualify for the subsidy.
