Qui est admissible

To be eligible to receive the CEWS, you must:

  • be an eligible employer, and
  • have experienced a [sufficiently] reduced revenue due to COVID-19

Who is an eligible employer

Eligible employers include:

  • individuals
  • taxable corporations
  • non profit organizations
  • registered charities
  • partnerships consisting of eligible employers

Public bodies are not eligible for the CEWS. This includes municipalities and local governments, Crown corporations, public universities, colleges, schools and hospitals.

What is an eligible revenue reduction

You must determine if your reduced revenue makes you eligible to apply for the CEWS in a particular period.

Calculate your reduction by comparing your eligible revenue for the starting month of the period with what you earned either in the corresponding month in 2019 or in January and February of 2020:

Eligibility periods for the Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy
Period dates Baseline revenue Eligibility period revenue Required reduction
March 15, 2020 to April 11, 2020
  • March 2019, or
  • Average of January and February 2020
 March 2020 15%
April 12, 2020 to May 9, 2020
  • April 2019, or
  • Average of January and February 2020
 April 2020 30%
May 10, 2020 to June 6, 2020
  • May 2019, or
  • Average of January and February 2020
 May 2020 30%

You can choose either to compare your revenue to the corresponding month in 2019 or to your average monthly revenue from January and February 2020. However, the method of comparison you choose must be the same for each period.

Eligible revenue

Eligible revenue generally includes revenue earned in Canada earned from arm's-length sources.

It does not include amounts from extraordinary items or amounts on account of capital.

Registered charities and non-profit organizations must choose whether or not to include revenue from government sources when applying for the CEWS. You must take the same approach for each period you are applying for.

There are special rules for revenue related to certain non-arm's-length transactions and affiliated groups. [Read more about how revenue is calculated when determining eligibility for the CEWS.]

Who are eligible employees

An eligible employee is an individual who:

  • is employed by you in Canada, and
  • has not gone without eligible remuneration for 14 or more consecutive days in the eligibility period

What is eligible remuneration

Eligible remuneration includes any amounts [for which you would normally withhold or deduct amounts to remit to the Receiver General on account of the employee’s income tax obligation]. This may include:

  • salary
  • wages, including [hazard or "hero" pay increases]
  • other remuneration, such as [tips]
Severance pay and items such as stock option benefits or the personal use of a corporate vehicle are not part of eligible remuneration. [For more detailed information about what is included in eligible remuneration...]
