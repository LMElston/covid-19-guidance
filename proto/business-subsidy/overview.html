How the wage and rent subsidies have changed

The CEWS and the CERS have ended effective October 23, 2021.

The CRHP has been extended to May 7, 2022.

Starting on October 24, 2021, you may qualify for support through the:

  • Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program (THRP) or the
  • Hardest-Hit Business Recovery Program (HHBRP)

Both the THRP and the HHBRP include a wage subsidy and a rent subsidy.

If your business activities are significantly affected by a qualifying lockdown, you may be eligible for support through the THRP even if you are not a tourism or hospitality business.

What subsidies you may apply for

If you are an eligible employer and have qualifying wage and payroll expenses, you may be eligible to apply for one of these subsidies:

  • THRP – wage portion or
  • HHBRP – wage portion or
  • Canada Recovery Hiring Program

If you are an eligible entity and have qualifying rent and mortgage expenses, you may be eligible to apply for one of these subsidies:

  • Tourism and Hospitality Recovery Program or
  • Hardest-Hit Business Recovery Program

You may apply for one wage subsidy portion and one rent subsidy portion if you have eligible expenses for both.

Example [Expand/hide? Or do we wait until further into the context of eligibility to provide various examples?]

Your business has eligible wage expenses and eligible rent expenses. Your business also has hiring expenses that are eligible for CRHP.

You:

  • are not a part of the tourism and hospitality industry
  • have not been affected by a qualifying lockdown
  • qualify as a hardest-hit business under the HHBRP

When you calculate your amounts for the wage subsidy portion:

  • if the CRHP offers a higher amount than the HHBRP, you may apply for the CRHP instead of the HHBRP wage portion
  • if the HHBRP offers a higher amount than the CRHP, you may apply for the HHBRP wage portion instead of the CRHP

Since your business has eligible rent expenses, you may also apply for the HHBRP rent portion.
