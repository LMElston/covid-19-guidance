“Tax year” on “Balance” table:

You can view account transactions by selecting the applicable tax year. All transactions, including arrears assessment, payments, penalties and interests, are displayed on the result page.

For prior years data or all tax years data, select “Customize view of transactions” on the side bar, then select “Time Frame” or “Date Range” to view details.

Amount paid

The Canada Pension Plan contributions, employment insurance premiums, and income tax deductions you paid for the year indicated.

Amount unpaid

The assessed amount which might include unpaid Canada Pension Plan contributions, employment insurance premiums, and income tax deductions.

Balance adjustment

The sum of adjustments to debits, credits and unpaid amounts that are owed in a specific tax year to balance the account.

Balance

The total of all payments of Canada Pension Plan contributions, employment insurance premiums and income tax deductions for the year indicated, plus any unpaid assessed amounts, less the “T4 type” information return amount and balance adjustment amount posted on your account.

Total amount owing

The total of unpaid assessments of Canada Pension Plan contributions, employment insurance premiums, and income tax deductions amount plus any assessed penalties and interest outstanding amount and, if applicable, payable administrative adjustments and law cost amount.

“Tax year” on “Arrears” table:

You can view account transactions by selecting the applicable tax year. Only arrears amounts, such as arrears assessment, arrears payments, penalties and interests, are displayed on the result page.

For prior years data or all tax years data, select “Customize view of transactions” on the side bar, then select “Time Frame” or “Date Range” to view details.

Amount owing

The sum of unpaid amounts, penalties, and interest for each tax year. For the 2013 and 2014 tax years, the penalties and interest are included in the “Prior years” field.

Uncharged interest

Interest amount not yet billed, but that will be billed shortly.

Law costs

Law costs are the costs for levying writs. The costs are fees payable to the Canada Revenue Agency as a result of legal action taken to collect a balance owing. Law costs are not necessarily associated with a particular filing period and, as such, are posted to the non-filing period. Interest is not charged on law costs.

Missing a payment or need assistance?

Electronic payments will be applied to your account in approximately 48 hours. It may take longer to process payments mailed or made at financial institutions. If you need assistance, request a call back.
