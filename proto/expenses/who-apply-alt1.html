COVID-19 examples

Working from home for at least a month (four consecutive weeks) due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Working situation

Kumi’s employer required her to work from home in April and May 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked exclusively from home and performed all the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Kumi was required to work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in her case, almost nine weeks) and she mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, she may be able to claim the home work space expenses she paid in that period if she meets the other conditions.

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all the duties she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Emma was required to work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in her case, 31 continuous weeks) and she mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during those weeks, she may be able to claim home work space expenses she paid in that period if she meets the other conditions.

Working situation

Jason is a part-time employee who works three days a week at his employer’s office. From mid-March to July 2020, Jason’s employer required him to work from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During those weeks, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Jason was required to work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in his case, 20 weeks) and he mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during those weeks, he may be able to claim home work space expenses he paid in that period if he meets the other conditions.

Working from home for less than a month (four consecutive weeks) due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all the duties he normally performed at his employer’s office. After the three-week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

unsuccessful Result

Because Isaac did work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in his case, he worked from home for only three continuous weeks), he cannot claim home work space expenses he paid in that period.
