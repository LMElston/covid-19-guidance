Critères d’admissibilité

To claim employment expenses for the use of a work space in your home, you must meet all of the following conditions:

  • Note: If you are working from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic, a written or verbal agreement is acceptable.

    • Types of work spaces

      Note: If you work from home during the COVID-19 pandemic for a continuous period of 4 weeks or more and mainly (more than 50% of the time) do your work from your work space in this period, you meet this condition. Refer to the COVID-19 examples

      or

    • What is the daily-rate claim

      To simplify the claim for your work space expenses and office supply, internet, and phone expenses due to the COVID-19 pandemic, you can claim $2 for each day of the eligibility period you worked from home, to a maximum of $400.

      or

    • What is a T2200 (Short)

      Form T2200 (Short), Declaration of Conditions of Employment for Working at Home During COVID-19 is a shorter version of Form T2200 that your employer uses if you were required to work from home in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Your employer fills out this form to certify that you met the conditions of employment and had to pay for home office expenses under your employment contract.

      or

    • What is T2200

      Form T2200, Declaration of Conditions of Employment is a form your employer fills out to certify you met the conditions of employment and had to pay for employment expenses under your contract.

    Note: Keep a copy of the T2200 (Short) or T2200, in case we ask for it.

You need all of the above to be eligible to claim working from home expenses

You are eligible to claim working from home expenses

COVID-19 examples

Working from home for 4 weeks or more due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all the duties she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Emma was required to work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in her case, 31 continuous weeks) and she mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during those weeks, she may be able to claim home work space expenses she paid in that period if she meets the other conditions.

Working situation

Jason is a part-time employee who works three days a week at his employer’s office. From mid-March to July 2020, Jason’s employer required him to work from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During those weeks, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Jason was required to work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in his case, 20 weeks) and he mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during those weeks, he may be able to claim home work space expenses he paid in that period if he meets the other conditions.

Working from home for less than 4 weeks due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all the duties he normally performed at his employer’s office. After the three-week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

unsuccessful Result

Because Isaac did work from home for 4 continuous weeks or more in 2020 (in his case, he worked from home for only three continuous weeks), he cannot claim home work space expenses he paid in that period.
