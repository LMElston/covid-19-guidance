What the changes are

This page describes the recent changes to claiming work space in the home expenses.

If you have never made this claim before, you can skip to the first decision you have to make, Compare the claim methods, or you can review this page to understand these changes.

On this page

  • New flat rate method
  • New options to claim the actual amounts you paid
  • New eligible expenses

New flat rate method

As an employee, you may be able to claim certain home office expenses (work-space-in-the-home expenses, office supplies, and certain phone expenses).

The new Temporary flat rate method simplifies your claim for home office expenses by allowing your to claim $2 for each day you worked at home (as long as you meet the eligibilty criteria).

Download infographic: Working from home?

Eligibility
Temporary flat rate method

If you worked more than 50% of the time from home for a period of at least a month (four consecutive weeks) in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, you can claim $2 for each day you worked from home during that period. You can then also claim any additional days you worked at home in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The maximum you can claim using the new temporary flat rate method is $400 per individual.

Each individual working from home who meets the eligibility criteria can use the temporary flat rate to calculate their home office expenses deduction. This means multiple people working from the same home can each make a claim.

This method can only be used for the 2020 tax year.

  • keep receipts

What counts as a work day
Temporary flat rate method

Days that can be counted

  • days you worked full-time hours from home within the eligible period
  • days you worked part-time hours from home within the eligible period

Days that cannot be counted

  • days off
  • vacation days
  • sick leave days
  • other leave or absence

New options to claim the actual amounts you paid

You can still choose to claim the actual amounts you paid, which is known as the detailed method.

Eligibility
Detailed method

You are eligible to claim expenses for the period you worked from home, if you meet all of the criteria:

  • you worked more than 50% of the time from home for a period of at least a month (four consecutive weeks) in 2020
  • have a completed and signed Form T2200S and Form T2200 from your employer

Simplified process
Detailed method

To support you, the CRA:

  • created a simplified Form T2200S and Form T777S
  • created a calculator to help you meet their reporting obligations
  • will accept an electronic signature on the Form T2200S and Form T2200 to reduce the necessity for taxpayers and tax preparers to meet in person (applies to the 2020 tax year only)

What counts towards a work space
Detailed method

Whether you work at the dining table or in a space bedroom, there are several factors to consider when calculting your work space use:

  • Size of your home and work space
  • Types of work spaces
  • Hours per week you use the space for work
  • Number of workers in the home
  • Change of work space

New eligible expenses

The CRA has expanded the list of eligible expenses that can be claimed to include:

  • internet costs
  • the utilities portion of condo fees
