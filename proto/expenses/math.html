How the claim is calculated

On this page

  • Temporary flat rate method
  • Detailed method

Temporary flat rate method

  • # of days you worked from home within the eligible period (maximum 200)
  • times by $2 (temporary flat rate amount allowed)
  • equals Amount of your claim (to a maximum of $400)

For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

  • Eligibility criteria - Detailed method
  • What counts as a work day at home

Detailed method

Steps

  1. Calculate your work space use

    You must first get the area of both your work space and the total finished areas of your home.

    • Area of work space (dedicated or shared)
    • divided by Total finished area of home
    • times 100 (to convert it into a percentage)
    • equals A% Total amount of home used for work space (rounded two decimal places)

    For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

    • Size of your home and work space
    • Types of work spaces
    If you are working in a dedicated room

    This is also your total amount used for work space and employment purposes, and can proceed to calculating your work space in home expenses.

    A% equals C%

    If you are working in a common (shared) space

    You need to provide the amount of hours worked in that space for within a calendar week (7 days or 168 hours).

    •    # hours (hours worked in a work week)
    • divided by 168 hours (total hours in a week)
    • times 100 (to convert it into a percentage)
    • equals B% Total amount of employment hours in your work space (rounded to two decimal places)

    You will now calculate your shared work space and employment use by multiplying these two numbers together.

    • A% Total amount of home used for work space
    • times B% Total amount of employment hours in your work space (rounded to two decimal places)
    • times 100 (to convert it into a percentage)
    • equals C% Total amount used for work space and employment purposes (rounded to two decimal places)

    For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

      • Hours per week you use the space for work

  2. Calculate your work space expenses

    You will need to first add up all your home expenses (where applicable) for the eligible periods you worked from home.

    • Total electricity, heat, water and internet
    • plus Maintenance costs for the home that include the work space
    • plus Home insurance (only commission employees who own their home)
    • plus Property taxes (only commission employees who own their home)
    • plus Other home expenses that include the work space (e.g. Rent)
    • equals $D Total amount of expenses for the home and work space

    For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

    • Work-space-in-the-home expenses
    • Limitations on work-space-in-the-home expenses

    You will now calculate a percentage of these expenses to claim based on what you determined to be your size work space and employment use.

    • $D Total amount of expenses for the home and work space
    • times C% Total amount used for work space and employment purposes
    • equals $E Total amount of home expenses you can claim for employment-use

    You must also calculate the expenses on maintenance for your work space only.

    For a dedicated room
    • Maintenance on work space only
    • times 100%
    • equals $F Total amount of work space expenses you can claim for employment use
    For a common (shared) space
    • Maintenance on work space only
    • times B% Total amount of employment hours in your work space
    • equals $F Total amount of work space expenses you can claim for employment use

    For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

    • Maintenance and minor repair costs

    Finally, you add the two expenses together.

    • $E Total amount of home expenses you can claim for employment-use
    • plus $F Total amount of work space expenses you can claim for employment use
    • equals $G Total amount of home expenses you can claim for employment-use
    If you are claiming other employment expenses

    If you are calculating the amounts for T777(20), you can stop here. The remainder of calculations must be done on the form.

    If you are calculating with the T777S (20), continue on to calculating your employment expenses.

  3. Calculate your employment expenses

    For the T777S (20), you will need to add together all supplies and other employment type expenses that are part of working from home, but not based on the space you work from.

    • Supplies (e.g. postage, stationery, ink cartridges, other office supplies, etc.)
    • plus Other expenses (employment use of a cell phone, long distance calls for employment purposes, etc.)
    • equals $H Total amount of employment expenses

    For a description of the variables in this math formula, refer to:

    • Office supplies and phone expenses

  4. Calculate your eligible income

    To determine if you have any expenses to carry forward and to calculate the final amount you can claim, you must provide you income amounts and certain deductions.

    • Employment income (box 14 of your T4)
    • minus $H Total amount of employment expenses
    • minus Union dues (lines 20700 from your return)
    • minus Registered Pension Plan (RPP) deductions (line 21200 of your return)
    • equals $I Total eligible income amount

  5. Calculate your carry forward amount

    To calculate any carry forward amounts, you must subtract your home expenses from your eligible income.

    • $G Total amount of home expenses you can claim for employment-use
    • minus $I Total eligible income amount
    • equals $J Total amount to carry forward

  6. Calculate the final amount you can claim

    To calculate the final amount you can claim, you must add up a few of previous amounts you have calculated.

    • $H Total amount of employment expenses
    • plus
      The lesser of either:
      $F Total amount of home expenses you can claim for employment-use
      $I Total eligible income amount
    • equals $K Total amount of work space in home expenses you can claim, which will go on line 22900 of your return
