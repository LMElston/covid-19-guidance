Daily-rate claim

Simplified option

To simplify your claim for work space and office supply expenses on your 2020 tax return, you can claim $x per day you worked from home, to a maximum of $x.

What counts as a work day at home

What days can be counted:

  • days you worked full-time hours from home
  • days you worked part-time hours from home

What days can't be counted:

  • days off
  • vacation days
  • sick leave days
  • other leave or absense days

Simplified for you

You do not have to:

  • keep receipts
  • complete a T777 (Short) or T777 form

Steps

  1. Confirm you are eligible

    You must meet the eligibility criteria to claim your expenses

  2. Determine how much you can claim

    After you count the number of days you have worked from home, multiple that by $x per day. This amount will be your claim (up to a maximum of $x).

    • # days (number of days you worked from home - maximum 200)
    • times by $x (daily claim amount allowed)
    • equals Amount of your claim (to a maximum of $x)

  3. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    Claim on Line 22900 "Other employment expenses" on your tax return

Tip: Use the calculator to determine if you may be able to claim a higher claim. If you want to claim the higher amount, you will need to follow the steps for making a standard claim.

Standard claim

Follow these steps when you are:

  • claiming more than $x on your 2020 tax return
  • claiming other expenses (vehicle expenses, accounting or legal fees, tools, or other allowable employment expenses) as well as work space and office supply expenses
  • filing 2019 and prior tax returns

Steps

  1. Confirm you are eligible

    You must meet the eligibility criteria to claim your expenses

  2. Fill in the form

    Tip: Use the calculator to determine your personal-use expenses and work space use to claim on the T777 (Short) or T777 form

    Form for those working from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic: Option 1 of 2

    Use Form T777 Short - Statement of Employment Expenses for Working at Home Due to COVID-19, if you are claiming expenses related to working from home because of the COVID-19 pandemic

    Standard form: Option 2 of 2

    Use Form T777 - Statement of Employment Expenses if you need to include additional expenses like gas, vehicle, accounting fees, tools, and parking.

  3. Claim the deduction on your tax return

    Claim on Line 22900 "Other employment expenses" on your tax return

  4. Keep your records

    Keep the following records for six years:

    • Declaration of Conditions of Employment (Form T2200 (Short) or T2200)
    • any receipts, supporting documents and records
