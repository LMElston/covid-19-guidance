Provincial and territorial restrictions

There are travel restrictions and exemptions when moving between or within some provinces and territories.

  • Entering Canada from another country

Entering Canada from another country

If you entered Canada from another Country, you are subject to the federal requirements and possible additional provincial or territorial requirements.

When federal and provincial or territorial requirements are different

In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions to enter Canada and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with the regulation that is the most stringent.

If a province or territory has no additional requirements, you will only be subject to the federal requirements. Requirements are subject to change. Always verify with the province or territory before travelling to Canada.

Connecting flights within Canada

If you are cleared to fly, you may board a connecting flight to reach your final destination. You do not need to quarantine (self-isolate) before connecting, unless your final destination requires you to quarantine at a designated location first.

Travelling between provinces

Whether you drive or fly, if you travel between provinces without having been out of the country, there are no federal travel restrictions, but there may be provincial or territorial rules and restrictions.
