COVID-19 mandatory hotel stopover: Planning and booking

  • Plan and book
  • Traveller responsibilities
  • Hotel responsibilities

All travellers entering Canada, regardless of citizenship, must follow testing and quarantine requirements. This is to keep everyone safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world.

Now is not the time to travel. We strongly advise you to cancel or postpone non-essential trips.

Who has to do a hotel stopover

Flying to Canada

At this time, to prevent the spread of COVID, you must book and pay for a 3-night hotel stopover before you arrive in Canada. You will stay at the government-authorized hotel while you await your arrival test results.

This applies even if you have:

  • been vaccinated for COVID-19
  • recovered from COVID-19
  • tested negative for COVID-19 on your pre-entry test
  • travelled for compassionate reasons
  • a place to quarantine alone

Air travellers are only exempt from the 3-night hotel stay in of these situations:

  • an unaccompanied minor or unaccompanied dependent child
  • already exempt from the arrival test requirement
  • already exempt from the quarantine requirement

Temporary Foreign Worker Program exemptions

Until March 21, 2021, workers in certain seasonal occupations under the National Occupational Classification (NOC) system won’t need to complete the 3-night hotel stopover.

Instead, you'll go directly to the location of your 14-day quarantine provided by their employer. This location is where you'll wait for the results of your COVID-19 test taken on arrival.

More details will be available soon about the process that seasonal workers should expect after March 21, 2021.

Exempt occupations

Primary agriculture:

  • NOC 0821: Managers in agriculture
  • NOC 0822: Managers in horticulture
  • NOC 8252: Agricultural and related service agricultural service contractors, farm supervisors and specialized livestock workers
  • NOC 8255: Landscaping and ground maintenance: contractors and supervisors, landscaping, grounds maintenance and horticulture services
  • NOC 8431: General farm workers
  • NOC 8432: Nursery and greenhouse workers
  • NOC 8611: Harvesting labourers

Other agri-food occupations:

  • NOC 6331: Butchers, meat cutters and fishmongers (retail and wholesale)
  • NOC 9461: Process control and machine operators, food, beverage and associated products processing
  • NOC 9462: Industrial butchers and meat cutters, poultry preparers and related workers
  • NOC 9463: Fish and seafood plant workers
  • NOC 9617: Labourers in food, beverage and associated products processing
  • NOC 9618: Labourers in fish and seafood processing

Driving to Canada

At this time, you do not have to complete a hotel stopover if you're driving to Canada because many land border crossings are far from hotels in which travellers could quarantine.

You do have to follow other testing and quarantine requirements based on your situation:

  • Driving to Canada requirements checklist

Steps for booking your hotel

You can book your hotel up to 14 days in advance of your arrival. Book in the same city as the airport of your initial arrival into Canada.

You must ensure that you have a confirmed booking at a government-authorized hotel before boarding a flight coming to Canada. You could face a fine of up to $3,000 if you don't provide evidence of pre-paid accommodation at a government-authorized hotel prior to boarding.

Follow these steps:

  1. Have your travel details ready
  2. Choose and book your hotel
  3. Get a confirmation to show at the border

1. Have your travel details ready

To book your hotel, you must have:

  • traveller name(s)
  • date(s) of birth
  • gender (male, female, or undisclosed)
  • arrival city and date
  • payment information
  • special requests and accessibility concerns
  • pet information
  • email address
  • credit card information for pre-payment

2. Choose your hotel

Prices

Hotels set their own rates. Prices are subject to change.

The price will include costs associated with the:

  • food
  • room
  • security
  • transportation
  • infection prevention and control measures

You can book your hotel up to 14 days in advance of your arrival.

The mandatory 3-night reservation is:

  • fully pre-paid
  • non-refundable
  • charged to the credit card provided at the time of booking
Pets and dietary restrictions
  • If you have a pet, make arrangements to book your mandatory stopover in a hotel that allows pets
  • If you have dietary restrictions, discuss them with the hotel whne you call to book, and when you check in
People travelling together

People travelling together may share accommodation. Room rates will include base room rate plus an additional fee for each additional person staying in the same room.

Changing your reservation

Contact the hotel where you have a confirmed booking if you need to change your booking date.

3. Book your hotel

Direct booking

Most hotels offer direct online booking. For all others, you can call the hotel directly.

When booking, tell the hotel that you need a government-authorized room and rate to make sure you're booked correctly.

Central booking call line

You can also reserve and pay for your mandatory 3-night hotel stopover through GB Travel Canada Inc.:

  • 1-800-294-8253 (toll-free within North America)
  • 1-613-830-2992 (collect outside of North America)

Hours of operation: 8 am to 11 pm ET, 7 days a week. Calls received after hours will be limited to emergencies.

You should only call the hotel booking line when you are ready to reserve your room.

The phone line is receiving a high volume of calls.

Expect to receive an email confirmation within 4 hours of booking.

4. Get a confirmation from the hotel to show at the border

Print your confirmation or have the email or a screen shot ready to show to a government official at the border.
