COVID-19 mandatory hotel stopover: Plan and book

  • Plan and book
  • Staying at the hotel
  • Hotel responsibilities

Now is not the time to travel. We strongly advise you to cancel or postpone non-essential trips.

All travellers entering Canada, regardless of citizenship, must follow testing and quarantine requirements. This is to keep everyone safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world.

Who has to do a hotel stopover

Flying to Canada

At this time, to prevent the spread of COVID-19, you must book and pay for a 3-night hotel stopover before you arrive in Canada. You'll stay at the government-authorized hotel while you await your arrival test results.

This applies even if you have:

  • been vaccinated for COVID-19
  • tested negative for COVID-19 on your pre-entry test
  • a place to quarantine alone

Find out if your trip is exempt

Driving to Canada

At this time, you do not have to complete a hotel stopover if you're driving to Canada.

You do have to follow other testing and quarantine requirements based on your situation:

  • Driving to Canada requirements checklist

Choose and book your hotel

You must ensure that you have a confirmed booking at a government-authorized hotel before boarding a flight coming to Canada.

Border measures can change with little or no warning. It's strongly suggested that you book your hotel as close as possible to your arrival date in Canada.

If rooms aren't available for your preferred dates, you must change your flight for a date when a government-authorized hotel is available.

Sharing a hotel room

Only people who live in the same household may stay in the same hotel room if travelling together.

You could face a fine of up to $3,000 if you don't provide evidence of pre-paid accommodation at a government-authorized hotel prior to boarding.

Central booking line

You can book up to 14 days in advance of your arrival.

You can only book your mandatory hotel stopover:

  • directly with one of hotels listed
    or
  • by calling the central booking line

Reservations made through other travel agencies, online booking sites or apps will not be accepted. If you book via an online travel agency or app, you may be subject to a $3,000 fine for not having the appropriate booking.

If you prefer, you can also reserve and pay for your mandatory 3-night hotel stopover through the central booking line at GB Travel Canada Inc.:

  • 1-800-294-8253 (toll-free within North America)
  • 1-613-830-2992 (collect outside of North America)
  • Hours of operation: 8 am to 11 pm ET, 7 days a week. Calls received after hours will be limited to emergencies.

Expect to receive an email confirmation within 4 hours of booking.

Have your travel details ready

To book your hotel, you must have:

  • traveller name(s)
  • date(s) of birth
  • gender (male, female, or undisclosed)
  • arrival city and date
  • payment information
  • special requests and accessibility concerns
  • pet information
  • email address
Hotel prices

Hotels set their own rates. Prices are subject to change.

The price will include costs associated with the:

  • food
  • room
  • security
  • transportation
  • infection prevention and control measures

The mandatory 3-night reservation is:

  • fully pre-paid
  • non-refundable
  • charged to the credit card provided at the time of booking

Room rates include the base room rate plus an additional fee for each additional household member staying in the same room.

Changing your reservation

Contact the hotel where you have a confirmed booking if you need to change your booking date.

Get a confirmation from the hotel to show at the border

Print your confirmation, or have the email or a screen shot ready to show to a government official at the border.

Your hotel booking confirmation email will include a 6-digit alphanumeric trip reference code. You’ll need to input this trip reference code when you enter your trip information into ArriveCAN.
