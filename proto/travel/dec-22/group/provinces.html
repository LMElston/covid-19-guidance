Provincial and territorial restrictions

There are currently travel restrictions and exemptions when moving between some provinces and territories. Exemption reasons (such as for essential workers, family members, property owners, transiting passengers etc.) vary by destination.

When federal and provincial or territorial requirements conflict

In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions to enter Canada and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with the regulation that is the most stringent.

If a province or territory has no additional requirements, you will only be subject to the federal requirements. Requirements are subject to change. Always verify with the province or territory before travelling to or within Canada.

Connecting flights within Canada

If you are cleared to fly, you may board a connecting flight to reach your final destination. You do not need to quarantine (self-isolate) before connecting, unless your final destination requires you to quarantine at a designated location first.

Northern travel

Travel is being limited to prevent the spread of COVID-19. You may require documents, and may be required to quarantine before reaching your final destination.

Atlantic province "travel bubble"

Participating provinces have agreed to form a “travel bubble” together. This means they are permitting travel between a specific group of provinces. They are restricting entry for people from provinces and territories that are not part of the “bubble.” You may require documents to declare your arrival.
