Flying to Canada

The COVID-19 pandemic restricts travel to Canada and within Canada. It is essential to understand and comply with the requirements.

If you have determined you can enter Canada, use the checklist to complete and understand all the requirements.

Complete the Flying to Canada requirements checklist REMOVE TO NEXT SECTION - EVERYTHING HERE IS IN THE CHECKLIST

International travellers, regardless of citizenship, must:

  • plan your mandatory 3-night hotel stay
  • follow instructions for pre-board COVID-19 molecular test
  • submit their information using ArriveCAN
  • comply with a boarding flight check

In the coming days, you will be required to:

  • book your mandatory 3-night hotel stay before your flight
  • take a COVID-19 molecular test on arrival at the airport in Canada
  • go to your reserved hotel for 3 nights while you await your arrival test results
  • if your arrival test is negative, move from the hotel to complete the rest of your quarantine in either:
    • a designated quarantine facility or
    • your suitable place of quarantine at your final destination in Canada
  • toward the end of your quarantine take another COVID-19 test, following the instructions provided to you
  • stay in your place of quarantine while you await the results from this test

International arrivals and departures

International passenger flights arrive and depart at the following four Canadian airports:

  • Calgary International Airport
  • Montréal-Trudeau International Airport
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport
  • Vancouver International Airport

Private/Business and charter flights from all countries are required to land at the four airports. Flights from Saint-Pierre-et-Miquelon and cargo-only flights remain exempt.

Scheduled commercial passenger flights arriving from the United States, Mexico, Central America, the Caribbean and South America are also required to land at the four airports.

Mandatory 3-night hotel stay

In the coming weeks, travellers will be required to reserve a room in a hotel for 3 nights at their own cost, while they await their test results.

Before boarding a flight to Canada

If you’ve determined you can travel to Canada at this time, you must use ArriveCAN to submit your information before you board your flight.

Transiting passengers who are only passing through Canada to reach another country don’t need to use ArriveCAN.

Requirements to board flights to and within Canada

All existing mandatory pre-boarding and health requirements for air travellers remain in place, including temperature screening, health check questions, and wearing masks on board flights to and within Canada.

Travellers won’t be allowed to board if they:

  • have symptoms of COVID-19 unless they have a medical certificate stating that their symptoms are not COVID-19 related or
  • have been refused boarding in the past 14 days due to a medical reason related to COVID-19 or
  • are subject to a provincial or local public health order

All air travellers must wear a non-medical mask while travelling, except:

  • children under 2 years old
  • people who are unable to remove the mask without assistance
  • people who provide a medical certificate certifying that they are unable to wear a face mask for a medical reason

In addition, foreign nationals won’t be allowed to board a flight to Canada if:

  • they’re travelling from a country other than the United States and are not covered by any of the exemptions in the Orders in Council or
  • they’re travelling from a country other than the United States for an optional and discretionary purpose
Requirements for public transportation to and from your flight

Operators of public transportation services may require travellers to wear a non-medical mask.

Before you travel, check for updates to see how transportation measures affect your plans and what you need to pack.

Connecting flights

If you’re cleared to fly, you will be allowed to board a connecting flight, without leaving the sterile transit area (the area in an airport where in-transit passengers are separated from other travellers), to reach your final destination in Canada.

Until the new measures come into effect, you generally do not need to quarantine (self-isolate) before taking a connecting flight, unless your final destination requires you to quarantine at a designated location first.

When the new arrival testing measures take effect, you will be required to stop over in the Canadian city in which you entered for a mandatory 3-night hotel stay. You must receive a negative test result before continuing your trip, and complete your quarantine at your final destination.

Mandatory hotel stay for all air travellers

For example: A Winnipeg bound international traveller flying into Vancouver, will stop over at hotel for up to 3 nights in Vancouver. After a negative test result, that traveller could take their flight to Winnipeg to go to their suitable place of quarantine.

You must wear a non-medical mask and practice physical distancing at all times.

Departing Canada

To limit the spread of COVID-19, the Government of Canada advises that you avoid non-essential travel outside of Canada and all travel on cruise ships outside Canada until further notice.

Foreign nationals leaving Canada should consult with the country of their destination.

Travellers with signs or symptoms

If you have signs or symptoms of COVID-19 you will be required to isolate. You will not be allowed to use public transportation to travel to the place where you must isolate.

You will not be allowed to board any public flight until:

  • 14 days have passed or
  • you present a medical certificate confirming that your symptoms are not related to COVID-19
