Apply for an exemption for compassionate reasons - requirements

Step 1: Determine what you need to attach to your request

If you're entering Canada to:

Be present during the final moments of a loved one’s life

You need to submit:

  • a completed Letter of Required Support
  • a completed Site Visit Authorization Form(s) if you’re requesting limited release from quarantine to be present, or if you will quarantine with the person
Provide care or support for someone

You need to submit:

  • a completed Letter of Required Support
  • a completed Site Visit Authorization Form(s) if you’re requesting limited release from quarantine to provide care, or if you will quarantine with the person you’re caring for
Attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

You need to submit:

  • proof of death
  • a completed Site Visit Authorization Form(s) if you’re requesting limited release from quarantine for the event, or if you will quarantine with others who are grieving
    • you do not need a Site Visit Authorization Form if the event will take place in a public outdoor setting, for example, a ceremony in a public park.

Canadians who can complete their quarantine

Canadians who will quarantine where they will support, care or grieve

You will need to submit a complete request for exemption online if your quarantine location is also where:

  • you’ll be present during the final moments of a loved one’s life
  • you’re caring for a person with a medical condition or supporting a critically ill person
  • the funeral or end-of-life ceremony will take place
Canadians who can complete quarantine before they support, care, or grieve

You won't have to request an exemption online if you can complete your quarantine plan at a separate location.

Canadians entering for compassionate reasons

Step 2: Complete the attachments

Before you start your request for exemption form, you must have completed and signed the form(s), letter or proof for your situation.

You must attach the standard Letter of Required Support and/or Site Visit Authorization form. We will not accept any other format.

Site Visit Authorization Form

Private sites

You need to attach a signed Site Visit Authorization Form from the administrator, proprietor or homeowner of each site or location you plan to visit during your limited release from quarantine, such as:

  • hospitals
  • funeral home
  • place of worship
  • retirement home
  • long-term care facility
  • palliative care facility, such as a hospice
  • private residence where the death or end of life ceremony is occurring or where you're supporting a critically ill person or caring for a person with a medical condition

Public outdoor sites

You don't need a Site Visit Authorization Form if you're visiting a public outdoor setting or site, for example, a ceremony in a public park.

Provincial and territorial restrictions

Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements.

Some provinces and territories may not allow release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada, a province or territory may have additional restrictions. In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with those that are the most stringent.

Check back regularly as these restrictions can change.

Instructions to request authorization to visit a site during quarantine

  1. The form can only be filled out by the site(s) administrator, proprietor or homeowner.
  2. Send the PDF form to the person in charge of the site(s) you will visit. They need to fill out and sign the form and send it to you digitally.
    • You can download and attach the fillable form: Site Visit Authorization Form (PDF) or
    • You can copy and paste this link into your email to the person in charge
      • https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/phac-aspc/documents/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/latest-travel-health-advice/compassionate-entry-limited-release-from-quarantine/site-visit-authorization-form-en.pdf
  3. The form can be filled out and signed on a computer with Adobe Acrobat.
  4. Have them send the file back to you to attach to the online request form. Print a paper copy to bring along when/if you enter Canada.
  5. Every traveller in your group will require Site Visit Authorization Form(s) with their name, including minors.
Letter of Required Support

You'll need the completed and signed letter of required support to submit your completed application to us for processing.

  1. The form can only be filled out by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada.
  2. Send the PDF form to the licensed health care practitioner in Canada who can certify that your presence is required. They need to fill out and sign the form and send it to you digitally.
    • You can download and attach the fillable form: Letter of Required Support or
    • You can copy and paste this link into your email to a licensed health care practitioner in Canada.
      • https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/phac-aspc/documents/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/latest-travel-health-advice/compassionate-entry-limited-release-from-quarantine/letter-of-required-support-form-en.pdf
  3. The form can be filled out and signed on a computer with Adobe Acrobat.
  4. Every traveller in your group will requires their own Letter of Required Support, including minors.

If you need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons during your quarantine period, you will also require a Site Visit Authorization Form(s).

Proof of death

As a foreign national, if you request to enter Canada attend a funeral or end of life ceremony, you require proof of death. The following documents are examples that can be used as part of the death notification:

  • burial permit
  • death certificate
  • statement of death
  • medical certificate of death
  • letter from the funeral home

If the funeral or end of life ceremony will occur during your quarantine period, you will require a Site Visit Authorization Form(s) in order to attend.

It's an offence under the Quarantine Act to provide a false or misleading statement, which could lead to up to:

  • 6 months in prison and/or
  • $750,000 in fines

Step 3: Complete your request for exemption with the attachments

Start the request for exemption form only once you have the appropriate documents for your situation completed, signed and ready to attach. (Site authorization form, Letter of support, or Proof of death)

Your request will not be accepted if it is incomplete.

Tips for the request for exemption form

  • You won't be able to save the form for completion later
  • There are 8 pages in total
    • clicking 'Next' to get to the following page doesn't submit information or your application
  • Have your attachment files ready to attach to the request on page 6
  • On the final page, click the Submit button
    • You'll receive an immediate message indicating, 'Submission completed successfully'

Continue your request for exemption online

You need to complete a new request for each applicant, including minors.

Wait for approval

If your submission is completed, you will also receive a receipt email with an application number.

Your application will be assessed by the Public Health Agency of Canada. We'll confirm that your request meets the requirements for entry and/or for limited release from mandatory quarantine for compassionate reasons.
