Apply for an exemption for compassionate reasons - required forms

Step 1: Fill and have signed the appropriate forms, letters and proof

Before you start your request for exemption form, you must have completed and signed the form(s), letter or proof for your situation.

Site authorization form: to temporarily leave your place of quarantine for a funeral, visit or ceremony

You will need to attach a signed Site Authorization Form from each site you're visiting during your quarantine period.

  • Send the Site Visit Authorization Form link to the administrator, proprietor or homeowner of each site or location you need to visit during your limited release from quarantine, such as:
    • hospitals
    • funeral home
    • place of worship
    • retirement home
    • long-term care facility
    • palliative care facility, such as a hospice
    • private residence where the death or end of life ceremony is occurring or where you're supporting a critically ill person or caring for a person with a medical condition

    • The Site Visit Authorization Form is mandatory and requires a signature by the person in charge of the location. This can be an administrator, proprietor or homeowner of the site.

    A Site Visit Authorization Form isn't required if you're visiting a public outdoor setting or site, for example, a funeral in a public park.

    Some provinces and territories may not allow for limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada, a province or territory may have additional restrictions. In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with those that is are the most stringent.

    Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements and check back often to find out if any of their quarantine requirements prohibit limited release from quarantine.

    Letter of Required Support: for non-Canadians to provide support or care for someone, or to be present during the final moments of life

    As a foreign national requesting an exemption to enter Canada, you will require a Letter of Required Support.

    1. Download the Letter of Required Support Form (PDF).
    2. Send the letter of required support to the licensed health care professional who can certify that your presence is required.
    3. You'll need the completed and signed letter of required support to submit your completed application to us for processing.

    Each applicant requires their own letter of required support.

    If you need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons during your quarantine period you will also require a Site Visit Authorization Form(s).

    Proof of death: to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

    As a foreign national, if you request to enter Canada attend a funeral or end of life ceremony, you will require proof of death. The following documents are examples that can be used as part of the death notification:

    • burial permit
    • death certificate
    • statement of death
    • medical certificate of death
    • letter from the funeral home

    If the funeral or end of life ceremony will occur during your quarantine period, you will require a Site Visit Authorization Form(s) in order to attend.

    Step 2: Filling out the request form

    Start the request for exemption form once you have the appropriate form, letter or proof of death completed and signed and ready to attach. Your request will not be accepted if it is incomplete.

    Tips for the request for exemption form

    • You won't be able to save the form for completion later.
    • There are 8 pages in total. Clicking 'Next' to get to the following page doesn't submit your application or save your information.
    • Attach the signed Letter of Required Support Form or Proof of death and a signed Site Authorization Form(s), if applicable, on page 6.
    • On the final 8th page, click the Submit button.
      • You'll receive an immediate message indicating, 'Submission completed successfully.'
    • Start a new request for exemption form for each traveller, including dependent children.
