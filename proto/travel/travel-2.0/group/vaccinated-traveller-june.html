    Starting July x: Exemptions for fully vaccinated travellers

    Travel restrictions on who can enter Canada remain in place. If you can enter Canada and you meet the criteria as a fully vaccinated traveller, some exemptions would apply:

    Pre-entry test required
    Arrival test required
    Exempt from hotel stopover
    End quarantine following negative arrival test result

    In ArriveCAN, you will be asked:

    • the date, country and vaccine you received for your first dose
    • if you received a 2nd dose and the same details for that dose
    • lastly, upload proof of your vaccinations, a photo or photos, or a PDF file with receipts or confirmations from the vaccinating
    Requirements for fully-vaccinated status follow. Final determination of your status will be made at the border - bring a copy of all proof of vaccination with you.

    Authorized vaccines for proof of vaccination

    At this time, Canada is accepting vaccines authorized by Health Canada.

    Authorized vaccines for fully-vaccinated status:

    • Pfizer (Comirnaty, tozinameran, BNT162b2)
    • Moderna (mRNA-1273)
    • AstraZeneca (Vaxzevria, AZD1222, Covishield)
    • Janssen (Johnson & Johnson) - single dose
    • Bharat Biotech (Covaxin, BBV152 A, B, C)
    • Cansino (Convidecia, Ad5-nCoV)
    • Gamalaya (Sputnik V, Gam-Covid-Vac)
    • Sinopharm (BBIBP-CorV, Sinopharm-Wuhan)
    • Sinovac (CoronaVac, PiCoVacc)
    • Vector Institute (EpiVacCorona)
    • Other

    Doses and dates for fully-vaccinated status

    To be considered fully-vaccinated, you must have:

    • received 2 doses of any combination of Pfizer, Moderna or AstraZeneca vaccines or
      • one dose of Janssen (Johnson & Johnson) vaccine
    • received your 2nd or only dose at least 14 days before your entry into Canada

    Uploading proof of your vaccination in ArriveCAN

    In ArriveCAN, you'll be asked to upload your proof of vaccination. The receipt(s) must be in English or French, or a certified translation (LF:what is a 'certified' translation?).

    The proof(s) must show clearly show:

    • your name and birth date
    • the date(s) you received the vaccine(s)
    • the vaccine(s) you received

    You can upload a:

    • PDF file or files - after your vaccination, you may have received a PDF receipt or confirmation from a government agency.
      • For example, in most Canadian provinces, a PDF receipt was emailed to vaccine recipients. Save the attached file to your desktop and upload it.
    • photo or photos of your receipt(s)
      • for example, upload a file that is a photo of your CDC card, or of the confirmation you received from the organization or government that provided your vaccine
      • Hint: if you aren't sure how to find the filename of a photo, most smartphones have an easy way to email a photo. Email it to yourself, and save the attachment in the email to your desktop with a filename you can easily find again. Then when you click 'Upload' in ArriveCAN, you'll know where to find the file, and the file's name.
    • the ArriveCAN mobile app: you can use your phone's camera from the app to take a photo of your receipt(s)
