Repayment relief for self-employed people

Some self-employed individuals who applied for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) were told they had to repay it because they had based their eligibility on their gross self-employment income. If these individuals met all the other eligibility criteria, they may not have to pay back the CERB. If they have already repaid it, they may be able to get their benefit reimbursed.

Repayment relief criteria

You may not have to repay the CERB if you meet all of the following conditions. If you have already repaid it, you may be able to get it returned.

You must meet all of the above criteria to be eligible for repayment relief. If you do not meet the above criteria, you must return or repay the CERB.

If you meet all the above criteria, you will not have to repay the CERB. If you have already repaid it, you should be able to get it back. be eligible for repayment relief.

If you meet the criteria but already repaid

If you meet the relief criteria

There is no action to take. You do not need to contact the CRA to check if you can keep the CERB. The CRA will review your 2019 and 2020 tax returns and contact you by mail if we need more information.

Repayment relief is a targeted resolution for a specific group of individuals. You can no longer apply for additional CERB periods retroactively. CERB applications are closed.

If you meet the criteria but already repaid

If you meet all the above criteria but have already repaid some or all of your CERB, you may be able to get it reimbursed. The CRA will contact you by mail with instructions on how to get your benefit back.

You must file your 2019 and 2020 income tax return (using your current T4A tax slip) before the CRA can process your reimbursement.

If you are reimbursed for a repayment you made in 2020, the CRA may reassess your 2020 tax return to reflect the all payments you received for that year.

You may expect to receive reimbursements starting in summer 2021.

If you do not meet the relief criteria

You will have to return or repay the CERB payments you received.
