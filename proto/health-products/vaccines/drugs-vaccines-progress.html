Securing potential vaccines and supplies

To date, Canada has invested over $1 billion in vaccine agreements. All Canadians can rest assured that a safe and effective vaccine will be available to them – for free – once it is ready.

The government has put in place an agile process that allows for review on a rolling basis. Health Canada's scientific reviewers are already evaluating data for at least 3 vaccines. This will ensure promising vaccine candidates receive timely review and authorization when found to be safe and effective.

Vaccination roll-out preparedness

In preparation for vaccine roll-out, the government has invested $284.2 million to improve our capacity to distribute COVID-19 vaccines to Canadians. Actions include:

  • Agreements to purchase syringes, alcohol swabs and other immunization supplies byPublic Services and Procurement Canada
  • Securing specialized freezer capacity to hold tens of millions of doses of specific vaccine candidates
  • Planning of options for shipping and storage of COVID-19 vaccines
  • Logistics planning by the Canadian Armed Forces and the Public Health Agency of Canada

Rolling out vaccines will be a complex national endeavour. As part of continuing preparations for the distribution of a vaccine, Canada is working with provinces, territories, Indigenous partners and private sector partners to ensure the appropriate planning and infrastructure is in place to receive and deploy vaccines.

Supported by: the COVID-19 Vaccine Task Force , the COVID-19 Therapeutics Task Force, and the Immunity Task Force.

Drugs and treatment progress in Canada

There are many potential drugs being investigated in Canada and around the world for use against COVID-19. Drugs must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they are authorized for use in Canada. Health Canada also monitors real-world evidence while they are on the market.

Clinical trials of drugs in Canada

Potential COVID-19 drugs currently being investigated in authorized Canadian clinical trials include:

  • antiviral drugs
  • anti-malarial drugs
  • monoclonal antibodies and other drugs that address inflammation
  • convalescent plasma collected from patients who have recovered from COVID-19
  • medical gases

Vaccines and drugs are safe to use and crucial to protecting Canadians

The Government of Canada has strict regulations and testing requirements to ensure that authorized vaccines and drugs are safe to use. We continue to monitor and assess the safety and effectiveness of a product once it is on the Canadian market

Working with international partners to ensure vaccines are safe and widely adopted

Canada is working with international partners on a coordinated and well-aligned approach to this global pandemic. This ensures that health products are safe, effective and quickly available to Canadians. Collaboration also helps advance the development of diagnostics, treatments and vaccines that will save lives and protect the health and safety of people everywhere.
